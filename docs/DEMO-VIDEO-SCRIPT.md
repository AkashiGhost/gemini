# InnerPlay — Demo Video Script
## Gemini Live Agent Challenge 2026

**Target Duration:** 3:00–3:30 (hard stop at 4:00 — only first 4 min evaluated)
**Platform:** YouTube or Vimeo (must be public)
**Tone:** Cinematic, calm, slightly tense. Not a product pitch — an invitation.
**Music:** Ambient, low drone. Think Hans Zimmer "Time" but quieter. No beats until the architecture section. Fade out on close.

## DIRECTOR'S NOTES

### The Central Constraint
The most important rule: **do not explain what the audience can see.** If the Gemini voice is playing, shut up. If the player is reacting, shut up. The product IS the experience — get out of its way.

### On the Live Audio Clip (0:38–1:00)
This 22-second window determines whether judges believe InnerPlay works. Guidelines:
- Use a real session capture — no re-dubbing, no scripted player responses
- Pick a moment where the AI clearly responds to something the player said and takes the story somewhere unexpected
- "Exit Interview" tends to produce stronger first-impression moments (the corporate horror framing is immediately legible)
- If the audio quality is rough, a light EQ pass is acceptable — but do not replace the AI voice

### On the Player
- Cast someone who hasn't played before if possible — genuine first-time reactions read as genuine
- If using yourself: record the session first, then film yourself listening to playback with eyes closed. The reaction will be authentic because the experience was real.
- Headphones matter visually: over-ear, preferably dark-colored. They signal "this is an audio experience" without saying it.

### Music Direction
- **0:00–1:30:** Near silence. A single low drone at -30dB, barely present. The Gemini audio must dominate.
- **1:30–2:15:** Bring music up to -18dB. Still ambient, no melody. A sense of momentum building.
- **2:15–3:00:** Subtle forward motion. A single repeating figure, like a clock. Architecture = forward progress.
- **3:00–3:35:** Back to near silence. End where you started.
- Do NOT use upbeat, startup-pitch music. This is not that kind of product.

### What NOT to Show
- Do not show a terminal or code editor (save that for the GCP deployment proof video)
- Do not show the Supabase dashboard or any admin UI
- Do not show a loading state or any error — edit around it
- Do not show a mouse cursor navigating menus for more than 3 seconds total

### Competitive Framing (Say It Once, Clearly)
The line at 1:20: *"AI Dungeon and similar games are screen-first. InnerPlay is the first game designed for the opposite."*
Say it once. Do not repeat or elaborate in the video. The contrast is self-evident once the audience has seen the player with eyes closed.
